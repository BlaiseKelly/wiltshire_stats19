STATS19 reported road casualties: LA region of Wiltshire

Published

March 5, 2026

Introduction

This is a report for the Local Authority (LA) region of Wiltshire, which can be reproduced for any LA by cloning the repository at https://github.com/blaisekelly/stats19_stats. #The LA boundary is shown on this plot of the cycle network (from OSM data processed with the osmactive package).

In this report all serious and slight casualty data is based on the adjusted data.

In 2024, Wiltshire had 879 collisions involving all casualty types resulting in 1179 casualties reported to the police. This is the 5th highest of 374 Local Authorities in Great Britain (96th per capita) and a decrease in collisions of 68 but a decrease in total casualties of 158. For fatal it ranked 12th ( 91st per capita) with 16 casualties down on 2023. For KSIs it ranked 6th (126th per capita) with 252 casualties down on 2023. For serious injuries it ranked 7th (133rd per 1000) with 236 casualties down on 2023. For slight injuries it ranked 5th (86th per 1000) with 927 casualties down on 2023.

Compared to 5 years ago collisions are up by 207, total casualties up by 310, fatalities down by 3, KSI casualties up by 93, serious injuries up by 96 and slight injuries up by 217.

For cyclists there were 75 collisions resulting in 75 cyclist casualties reported to the police. This is the 48th highest of 374 Local Authorities in Great Britain (175th per capita) and a decrease in collisions of 17 but a decrease in total casualties. For fatal it ranked 6th (46th per capita) with 2 cyclist casualties down on 2023. For KSIs it ranked 30th ( 138th per capita) with 26 casualties up on 2023. For serious injuries it ranked 38th (146th per 1000) with 24 cyclist casualties up on 2023. For slight injuries it ranked 54th (186th per 1000) with 49 cyclist casualties down on 2023.

Compared to 5 years ago collisions involving cyclists are down by 34, total casualties down by 36, fatalities up by 1, KSI casualties down by 6, serious injuries down by 7 and slight injuries down by 30.

For pedestrians there were 110 collisions resulting in 113 pedestrian casualties reported to the police. This is the 40th highest of 374 Local Authorities in Great Britain (180th per capita) and an increase in collisions of 12 and an increase in total casualties. For fatal it ranked 80th (166th per capita) with 2 pedestrian casualties down on 2023. For KSIs it ranked 52nd (222nd per capita) with 31 casualties up on 2023. For serious injuries it ranked 50th (215th per capita) with 29 pedestrian casualties up on 2023. For slight injuries it ranked 38th (153rd per capita) with 82 pedestrian casualties up on 2023.

Compared to 5 years ago collisions involving pedestrians are up by 42, total casualties up by 42, fatalities up by 1, KSI casualties up by 10, serious injuries up by 9 and slight injuries up by 32.

Speed Limits: Casualty rates by road speed limit

In Wiltshire, according to OSM data the majority of roads are 30mph (17%). Between 2020 and 2024 the majority of collisions (1588) happened on 30mph roads. (51)% of fatalities happened on 60mph roads, which make up 17% of roads in the LA. 484 casualties were seriously injured on 60mph roads. Collision rates per km are highest for 40mph roads, fatality rates highest on 40mph roads, serious injury rates highest on 50mph and slight injury rates 40mph. The collision rate on 20mph roads was 38.6% lower than the highest roads (40mph) and fatality rates 50% of 30mph roads and 14.3% of the highest fatality rate (40mph) roads. KSI rates were highest on 50, 60mph roads, 2.3 times higher than 20mph roads.

Relationship with Index of Multiple Deprivation Decile (IMD)

The Index of Multiple Deprivation (IMD) is the official measure of relative deprivation in England and is part of a suite of outputs that form the Indices of Deprivation (IoD) given as a decile in the STATS19 casualty and vehicle data for the casualty and drivers home LSOA respectively. It follows an established methodological framework in broadly defining deprivation to encompass a wide range of an individual’s living conditions. It is important in road traffic collision analysis because it says something about where harm is distributed in society. In Wiltshire, 73.7% of the population fall into the least deprived IMD decile. 67.4% of casualties injured between 2020 and 2024 fall into the least deprived IMD decile. 95.3% of casualty data had home LSOA.

IMD and Casualties

The relationship between deprivation and road casualty risk is marked in Wiltshire. The most deprived IMD decile (decile 1) accounts for 1.7% of casualties (94), while the least deprived (decile 10) accounts for 10% (551). Residents of the most deprived areas are 0.2 times more likely to be a casualty than those in the least deprived decile. The decile with the highest number of casualties overall is decile 8 with 51 casualties (0.9%). As noted above, 95.3% of casualty records had a home LSOA that could be matched to an IMD decile, where as only xx of driver data had data on home location.

The charts below show a breakdown of the ages and sex number of KSI and IMD Decil of casualties in Wiltshire.

Analysis of KSI casualties by IMD decile in Wiltshire reveals that decile 8 (least deprived) accounts for the highest KSI total across casualty types combined, with approximately 187 KSI casualties. Car occupants represent the highest KSI casualty type overall (~549). Pedestrian KSI peaks in decile 6, while cyclist KSI is highest in decile 8. By age, the most affected groups are 70+ for pedestrians, 50-59 for cyclists, and 50-59 for motorcyclists. Males account for approximately 726 KSI casualties compared to 348 for females.

MSOA

Between LSOA and Local Authority are Medium Super Output Levels (MSOA). Because of their relatively consistent size across the country, they are a useful unit for analysing data about small local areas and they also have been allocated descriptive names by the House of Commons Library makming it easier to describe areas. These areas have been used to analyse casualties by MSOA region and compare at a country level, similar to as has been done with LAs at the beginning of this document, but hopefully providing more targeted information for a local area.

Analysing the population weighted IMD from LSOA for MSOA for different casualty types, the strongest trends are for Taxi occupant, E-scooter rider casualties as shown in the plot below.

The most deprived MSOA (by weighted IMD score) was Salisbury Bemerton (IMD 9), while the least deprived was Chippenham Pewsham (IMD 3.8). Cost of prevention

Government TAG methodology (described here and here allocates a cost of prevention to each collision. The costs are calculated initially based on casualty severity and then grouped by collision severity. For example, the cost of fatal collisions includes the cost of all casualties (including serious and slight casualties) that occur in fatal collisions. This can be matched in various ways to the collision data. The two methods used for the overall reporting in ONS is to simply match the collision severity recorded in the crash data to a single cost for slight, serious and fatal and also to match based on severity and road type. Using the same methods as in the ONS report, the chart below shows the total cost of prevention for the collisions Wiltshire.

The cost of collisions by vehicle involved and by speed limit are shown in the plot below. 60mph roads had the highest total cost with £239,270,998, 83.9% above 30mph. The lowest total cost were 20mph with £13,757,910

Dividing the total cost on each road by the number of collisions gives an estimate for the average cost of each collision on each road. The lowest (£79,525) were 20mph roads and the highest (£159,620) 60mph roads. 4% of total collisions took place on 20mph roads, but 3% of the total cost and 60mph roads had 35% of total collisions but 46% of the total cost. The cost of collisions on 60mph roads are, on average, 2 times higher than 20mph roads. The average cost of collisions on 30mph roads was 1 times higher than 20mph roads.

Single Vehicle Pavement Collisions

Between 2020 and 2024, nationally there were 9327 single-vehicle collisions where a pedestrian was struck on a footway or verge. 81% were people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 12% were people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters. Of these collisions there were 138 fatalities, 94% of these fatal collisions were with people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 1% died from collisions with people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters. A further 2300 serious injuries, 79% of the fatal collisions were with people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 13% from collisions with people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters.

In Wiltshire there were 74 reported incidents, resulting in 76 casualties. The vehicle type most commonly involved was Car with 47 collisions and 48 casualties. 85% of the vehicles in collisions were people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 7% were people riding pedal cycles and escooters. Of these collisions there were 0 fatalities, NaN% of these collisions were with people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and NaN% from collisions with people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters. A further 11 serious injuries, 82% of which were with people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 9% from collisions with people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters.
